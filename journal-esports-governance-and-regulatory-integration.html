13. Esports Governance and Regulatory Integration

Written by Aria Kenangan • June 25, 2026

Structural Mechanics

Following the formal recognition of Esports as a multisport event by the Indian government, the regulatory framework has shifted toward structured governance under the Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports. To transition competitive gaming from unstructured leagues to a regulated sport, governing bodies are standardizing tournament rules, implementing anti-cheating digital protocols, and establishing anti-doping regulations (focusing on stimulants and cognitive enhancers).

Data-Driven Metrics

  • Audience Scale: The Indian competitive gaming audience has expanded rapidly, supported by the widespread availability of low-cost 4G and 5G connections and affordable mobile devices.
  • Integrity Enforcement: Regional tournaments require players to run kernel-level anti-cheat software, reducing match-fixing and automated software assist risks.
  • Monetization Ingress: Media rights valuations for mobile esports tournaments have grown, attracting sponsorships from consumer tech brands and telecom operators.

Strategic Vector

The Ministry should establish national guidelines for player contracts to protect young competitors from predatory agreements and guarantee fair revenue sharing.
