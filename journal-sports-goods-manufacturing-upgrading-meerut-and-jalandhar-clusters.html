15. Sports Goods Manufacturing: Upgrading Meerut and Jalandhar Clusters

Written by Aria Kenangan • June 25, 2026

Structural Mechanics

The traditional sports goods manufacturing hubs in Jalandhar (Punjab) and Meerut (Uttar Pradesh) are transitioning from manual stitching to advanced materials and automation. To remain competitive with global manufacturing centers, local firms are adopting carbon-composite molding, automated leather cutting, and synthetic-bladder vulcanization technologies. Supported by MSME modernization schemes, this shift helps local firms manufacture FIFA and ICC-certified equipment for international markets.

[Raw Materials: Carbon Composites / Synthetic Polymers] 
    --> Automated High-Precision Laser Cutting & Molding 
    --> Robotic Quality & Stress Testing 
    --> [Export-Ready Certified Sports Equipment (FIFA / ICC Approved)]

Data-Driven Metrics

  • Export Expansion: Upgraded manufacturing lines in Meerut and Jalandhar have expanded India's sports goods exports, supplying equipment to major global markets.
  • Structural Precision: Replacing manual leather cutting with automated CNC laser cutters has reduced raw material wastage by 18% and improved product consistency.
  • Quality Certification: High-end athletic equipment from modernized local units meets the strict performance standards of global federations, including FIFA, the ICC, and the BWF.

Strategic Vector

The Ministry of Commerce should establish shared toolrooms and testing centers in these clusters to help small manufacturers certify their products without high capital expenditure.

Future Outlook

The development of India’s sports ecosystem is directly linked to the country's broader physical, economic, and digital progress. As the nation targets Olympic medal growth and works toward hosting major global sporting events, modernizing sports infrastructure is essential. This requires a balanced approach that combines data-driven talent identification, structured public-private partnerships, advanced sports science, and sustainable manufacturing upgrades. By treating sports not just as recreation but as a structured, tech-enabled economic sector, India can build a sustainable pipeline of athletic success. Securing this foundation is key to establishing a healthier, more active population and a competitive national sports economy on the global stage.
